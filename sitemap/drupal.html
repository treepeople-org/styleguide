  71. What is a Cistern? | TreePeople
    Our region faces longer periods of drought and more intense storms, but you can make a difference! Be part of the new LA landscape and install a cistern to fight drought and adapt to our new climate reality. Cisterns and rain barrels are both rain tanks used to store captured rainwater. Smaller tanks (rain barrels) typically store around 55 gallons of water. Larger tanks (cisterns) store 200 gallons or more. They can be both above or under ground. The average home cistern stores 800 - 1500 gallons.

    • Why It Matters | TreePeople
      LA is rich in natural beauty. But along the way, we've over-paved this paradise. Many LA neighborhoods are almost completely covered in asphalt and concrete and sorely lacking trees and nature.  On hot days, scorching surfaces bake in the heat, endangering people’s health. Our streams and rivers are paved over, so that when it rains, water can't soak into the ground where it could add to our aquifers. Instead, it runs off into our ocean, sweeping trash and pollution onto our beaches.
